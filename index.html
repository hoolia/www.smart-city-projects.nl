Additional Advantages

Some other public and financial advantages

  • Global Warming Reduction

     

    With minimal emissions, renewable energy sources offer a solution to reducing global warming. By augmenting their supply, we can substitute carbon-intensive sources and greatly decrease emissions.

  • Improved public health

     

    Air and water pollution, the root cause of many health issues, are non-existent with clean energy technologies. These technologies have been estimated to save public health costs of $74.6 billion annually.

  • Inexhaustible energy

     

    Renewable energy has been proven time and again to be capable of meeting a significant portion of future electricity demands, even with potential limitations taken into account.

  • Jobs and other
    economic benefits

     

    Renewable energy sources generate more jobs per unit of electricity compared to fossil fuels. Furthermore, the expansion of clean energy can also have positive economic ripple effects.

  • Stable energy prices

     

    Initial investments are necessary to construct renewable facilities, but once in operation, they have low ongoing costs, resulting in stable energy prices over time compared to fossil energy prices.

  • Reliability and resilience

     

    Solar panels, due to their distributed and modular design, are less susceptible to widespread failure and can function dependably even under conditions that would necessitate shutting down a fossil fuel plant.
